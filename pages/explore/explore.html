EXPLORE

The Second Realm invites you to discover Bitcoin through a vast and diverse array of data landscapes.

Learn everything you need to make the most of this digital journey.

REQUIREMENTS

To visit the Second Realm, you need a Meta Quest 3 virtual reality headset and a Touch Plus controller.

Meta Quest 3 Virtual Reality Headset

A VR headset with trackers supporting 6 degrees of freedom (6DoF) is essential, and a screen resolution of at least 2000 pixels (width) per eye is highly recommended.

We suggest increasing the resolution of the display panels if the manufacturer has lowered the default resolution.

For the Meta Quest 3, we recommend registering a developer account and installing Sidequest on a computer. After connecting the headset to a USB port on the computer, configure the following settings in SideQuest:

  • CPU and GPU Level: 4
  • Default Texture Size: 3072
  • Refresh Rate: 90Hz
  • Fixed Foveated Rendering Level: Medium

These settings will enhance your immersive experience.

Touch Plus controller

A single Touch Plus controller is required to pilot the dataship that navigates the Second Realm.

Thumbstick (normal mode)

  • Up: Vertical Translation (up)
  • Down: Vertical Translation (down)
  • Left: Yaw (Anticlockwise rotation around the vertical Y axis)
  • Right: Yaw (Clockwise rotation around the vertical Y axis)

Thumbstick (rotation mode)

  • Up: Pitch (Downwards rotation around the horizontal X axis)
  • Down: Pitch (Upwards rotation around the horizontal X axis)
  • Left: Roll (Anticlockwise rotation around the horizontal Z axis)
  • Right: Roll (Clockwise rotation around the horizontal Z axis)

 

Point to Select

Pointing the controller to a block at a distance of less than 500 units allows to select the block and to display related information. The block stays selected for a few seconds before being automatically deselected.

THE VIRTUAL HEAD UP DISPLAY

Your guide to the virtual assistant that supports you throughout your journey.


The virtual HUD consists of a central compass flanked by panel displays on either side.

Information Display Panel

This screen shows miscellaneous information and instructions, such as details about the currently selected block or guidance for a tour.

Blockchain Loading Progress Bar

This screen indicates the blockchain loading progress in Timelapse mode. It also displays the boundaries of the blocks within the Realm.

Speed Meter

Displays the ship's speed in units per second (units/s). Each Realm's data space is a cube with dimensions of 210,000 units.

Speed Mode Panel

The ship offers four speed modes. Mode 4 is ideal for long-distance travel, while Mode 1 is best for approaching a block closely.

GPS

A panel showing the ship's location in coordinates within the data space.

Compass

The most intricate part of the HUD, consisting of two components:

- A "spinning top" representing the ship's horizontal plane and vertical axis. Its color indicates the ship's alignment with the data space axes:

  • Green: The ship's vertical axis aligns with the data space's vertical axis.
  • Orange: The ship's vertical axis aligns with a non-vertical axis of the data space.
  • Red: The ship's vertical axis is not aligned with any data space axis.

- A white "truncated cube" marking the "origin" corner of the data space, always located at the center of the sphere. This helps locate the origin quickly, and its edges assist in aligning the ship's vertical axis with a data space axis.

TIPS

Enhancing Your VR Experience

Whenever possible, increase the resolution of your VR headset. The improvement in visual quality is significant and cannot be overstated.

Controls and Movement

The trigger and thumbstick act as accelerators for each axis. Releasing an accelerator allows movement to continue due to inertia, but it will gradually slow down due to friction.

The speed mode can function as an engine brake to help control your movement.

Embrace Movement

Constant movement is key to enjoying Point Clouds. Keep moving to fully engage with the experience.

Mastering Speed Modes

Approaching a block is possible but requires mastering the vehicle’s different speed modes.

Navigating the Second Realm

The Second Realm’s abstract, scale-free nature challenges your perception of scale. What appears as a small detail from a distance at high speed may reveal itself as a skyscraper-sized structure when viewed up close at low speed. Embrace slowness to appreciate the beauty of these details.

Aligning Your Dataship

When rotating your dataship, use the compass’s vertical axis as a guide, aligning it with an edge of the truncated cube.

Trust Your Instincts

Millions of years of human evolution have attuned your brain to horizontal and vertical lines. Avoid navigating the data space when your compass is red, as it indicates misalignment.

Known Issue with Quest Browser

The Quest Browser has a known bug that may cause the application to freeze when a hand controller is disconnected and automatically reconnected. This issue has been reported to Meta and appears to be resolved in the latest versions of the OS and browser.
